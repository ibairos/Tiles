Tiles Rules by Ibai Ros Iturgaiz

Overview

Welcome to Tiles! In this game, the objective is to match as many tiles as possible with their equals, trying to do it in the fastest way possible.

Game Rules

  • When your turn comes, you have to pick two matching tiles from the board. Be careful because there is a time limit, and if you go over it, not only will you lose your turn, but you will also lose points!
  • If you find two matching tiles, you will get points and another turn. Alternatively, if you do not pick the correct tiles, it will be your opponents' turn.
  • Be careful with bombs! If you run into one, not only will you lose your turn, but you will also lose some points too.

    • Game Options

    The game lets you pick among many options, making the experience highly customizable. Among these, we have the following:

    Columns and rows

    Numbers of rows and columns the game board. The resulting number of cards has to be even, for simplicity.

    The minimum and maximum columns are 3 and 9, respectively. For rows, the values are 2 and 5. and 5x9.

    Difficulty

    The player will be able to choose among 5 levels of difficulty. The difficulty level will be proportional to various parameters, among which are:

  • The points received for each matching tile.
  • The points subtracted for each exploded bomb.
  • The points subtracted each time the turn time is exceeded.
  • The intelligence of the computer (if the game is played against the computer).

    • Time cards stay up

    The player will be able to choose the time the cards stay up each time a pair is selected and thus no other card can be selected, even when the pair is incorrect.

    Theme

    The player will be able to choose among 3 different preloaded themes (movies, tv shows and music), or import a custom one if the player desires.

    For that, the user must select the Load Theme button and select a folder with at least 20 image files (the game will only load 20). Once the theme is loaded, the player can play with the selected theme. Warning: the images will be shown with a 11:16 (vertical) aspect ratio.

    Opponent

    The player will be able to choose among 3 different game modes:

  • One Player: The game will only have one player, and thus the objective will be to flip all the tiles as quickly as possible.
  • Two Players: The game will have two players that will play against each other with alternating turns. The objective will be to achieve a greater score than the opposing player.
  • Computer: The game will have two players, the user and the computer itself. As the difficulty level is increased, so will the intelligence of the computer, (the randomness of its choices will decrease).

    • Scoring system

    The scoring system of this game, as we have mentioned above, will depend proportionally on the level of difficulty chosen. The scores will have some base values, used in the first level of difficulty, which will increase with the level.

    The parameters depending on the difficulty are explained in the table below:

    Difficulty Points if correct Points if bomb Points if timer exceeded Prob. of random move by computer
    1 10 -3 -5 80%
    2 20 -6 -10 60%
    3 30 -9 -15 40%
    4 40 -12 -20 20%
    5 50 -15 -25 0% (deterministic)
